About Me

The story behind the code.

I grew up curious about how things connect — not just in biology or math in isolation, but at the intersection where data tells a story that saves lives. That curiosity led me to write my final-year thesis on classifying bone marrow myeloproliferative neoplasm diseases using machine learning — a problem where a 15% accuracy gain isn't just a benchmark win, it's a step toward better clinical outcomes.

I graduated with a 9.11 CGPA from Aliah University and immediately continued to Jadavpur University for my M.Tech, because I believe there's a lot more ground to cover. I'm genuinely excited by the challenge of making ML models that don't just perform in notebooks — but generalize, deploy, and scale.

Outside of research, I've competed in hackathons, participated in debates, and stayed active in the NDLI Club — because I believe good engineers are also curious communicators.

★ Thesis Project

Classification of Bone Marrow MPN Diseases

A full research pipeline to classify myeloproliferative neoplasm subtypes from NCBI genomic data. The challenge: noisy, high-dimensional biological datasets where a single misclassification has real clinical consequences.

Processed and normalized raw genomic datasets using R and Python pipelines
Performed Differential Gene Expression analysis to isolate key biomarkers
Built SVM and Autoencoder models for multi-class disease classification
Achieved +15% accuracy through targeted feature engineering
